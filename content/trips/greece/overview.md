This trip started on a cruise and ended in Athens, with ten days in between hopping the Cyclades on our own. We'd been to Greece before, briefly, but never to the islands. We went in early May -- deliberately off-peak -- and found a version of Greece that was sometimes chilly and occasionally frustrating, but mostly exactly what we'd hoped for.

The Cyclades are not all the same. That sounds obvious and it isn't. Santorini is spectacle -- arguably the most visually extraordinary place either of us has been -- but it has surrendered almost entirely to tourism. Paros feels like a place where actual Greeks still live. Milos is sleepy in a way that rewards slower travelers and defeats impatient ones. Naxos, which we only saw for six hours, made us wish we'd rearranged more of our itinerary around it.

> We almost skipped Naxos entirely. It was a six-hour stopover on the way to Paros. It became the island we most want to return to.

We ended in Athens with three nights and not enough time. Go figure.

# How the Trip Was Structured

The cruise deposited us in Athens. From there we flew to Milos and worked our way east and north before finishing in Athens:

Athens → Milos → Santorini → Naxos (6 hrs) → Paros → Athens

We did three inter-island ferries and two flights. The ferry overhead -- arriving early, repacking, transferring -- is real, and we'd caution against cramming in more than we did. That said, we also fit in Delos, Mykonos, and Antiparos as day trips without re-packing once, which is worth knowing.

# What We'd Tell You Before You Go

* Go in May, but know what you're trading. The crowds are manageable and the restaurants don't require reservations. What you're trading is beach weather -- the water is cold, the beach clubs aren't open yet, and some days the wind makes it feel more like early spring than Mediterranean summer. We'd make the same choice again, but go in knowing this.
* The ferry logistics are manageable but require attention. We used Seajets for most routes, booked a week ahead, and paid the premium for Club class -- worth it. Ferryhopper is useful for mapping routes; Seajets' own site is where you'll actually book. Don't forget to check in online 2-48 hours before departure or you'll be hunting for a desk at the port.
* Hire guides selectively, but hire them. Our Athens guide at the Acropolis gave us things we couldn't have found on our own. Our Naxos guide Alex made a six-hour visit feel complete. Delos without a guide cost us probably half the experience.
* The islands reward a home base. Five nights in Paros let us do day trips to Delos, Mykonos, and Antiparos without touching our luggage. Two nights in Milos felt like exactly enough. Three nights in Santorini is right for seeing sunsets from Oia, Fira, and Imerovigli separately -- which you should do.

Greece rewards travelers who resist the urge to see everything. The overhead of moving between islands is real, and the places that surprised us most -- Naxos, the marble village of Apeiranthos, the Mycenaean room in Athens -- were the ones we almost skipped.
