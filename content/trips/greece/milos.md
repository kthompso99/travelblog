
Milos is quieter than the famous Cycladic islands, and wears it well. The tourist infrastructure is modest — Pollonia, where we stayed, has a restaurant row barely 100 yards long. That smaller scale gives the island a pace that Santorini and Mykonos traded away long ago. If you're willing to rent a car and explore, Milos easily rewards two or three days.

There are two main towns in Milos that attract tourists.  Pollonia is quaint and attractive, while Adamantas (sometimes spelled Adamas) is the much busier ferry port. They are a 15-20 minute drive apart.

## Where We Stayed

We stayed at Tania Milos in Pollonia and *loved* it. It has a homey vibe, we were chatting often with Tania herself.  The location is excellent, with stunning water and sunset views from at least some rooms, and from the lovely breakfast. It was an 8 minute flat/easy walk from the Pollonia restaurant row.

![Pollonia, Milos, from the walk towards Tania Milos](images/Milos-02.jpg)

At Tania Milos, we had the “Deluxe Room with Full Sunset Sea View” which is the most expensive room in the hotel.  But at under $300 a night in shoulder season, the balcony sunset views made it feel like a bargain.

![Sunset from our balcony at Tania Milos](images/Milos-06.jpg)

## Getting Around
We rented a car, and it’s hard to imagine enjoying the island without one.  There were few taxis, and sites and beaches are pretty spread out.

Driving on Milos is mostly good, but don’t do what we did; we rented a car and the helpful employee ‘upgraded’ us to a bigger car.  There are a lot of narrow roads, so bigger isn’t necessarily better.  Also the rental agency told us you couldn’t take the car off-road.

> We imagine that everyone must ignore this (not that we’re recommending it!), because so many of the popular beaches to visit are, in fact, off-road.

Be careful near Plaka, there are some narrow lanes – don’t always optimize for ‘fastest’ route on Waze, it’ll take you on some roads that are 3” wider than your car, try to stay on the biggest/most major roads.

## Exploring Milos

One of the most popular things to do in Milos is a catamaran cruise around the island’s coastline.  We booked ours with Odysseus Cruises.  They cancelled at 7pm the night before due to lack of staffing. So we can't report on it firsthand.

It was worth driving to the capital Plaka to see where there’s a replica Venus de Milo – known as Aphrodite de Milos here, near where she was found – depending on how interested you are in art.  She has a great view of the water from her hill.

## Beaches of Milos
  
I made a quick map for myself [here](https://www.google.com/maps/d/edit?mid=10STMCKq4Tnfg4n-Z_B0SCEIKhQogt8I&usp=sharing) based on some online recommendations of “best beach in Milos”.
  
We especially recommend Sarakiniko beach which is a 5-8 minute walk downhill from the parking lot but one of the most unusual beaches we saw in the Cyclades. 

![Sarakiniko Beach](images/Milos-03.jpg)

  
Note that Milos is *very* hilly – so that for example it looks on the map that Fyriplaka beach is right near Paliochori beach, but there’s no “circle” road on this island, you drive 10 minutes back into the interior then back out to the next beach. The road down from the interior to Fyriplaka is hairy, you’re going down a steep road right near a quarry with massive trucks around you.
  
Many of these beaches have offroad components; when we rented our car we were told “not to drive offroad” or something like that … I can only assume most tourists ignore that, since many of the best beaches on Milos … require offroading (this was true in Paros as well, as an aside).

## Food and drink

Several locals told us Yialos is the best restaurant in the main waterfront row in Pollonia, we thought it was excellent. Some sites (TripAdvisor, others) list this as “Gialos’, but the menu very definitely had a Y. 

There is an Asian (sushi) restaurant in Pollonia called Hanabi, the locals seem unenthusiastic about it, but we enjoyed it as a change from the Greek fish restaurants which all have similar menus.

Medusa restaurant in Mandraki is well-known (various celebs have eaten there) and worth the detour for lunch (and probably dinner) – lovely views, nice food, and a 15-minute drive from Pollonia.

[Kostankis](https://kostantakiswinery.com/) winery is a 10-min walk from Pollonia – it was very good, super friendly young staff. It’s the only winery on Milos, and was a nice intro to local varietals.
