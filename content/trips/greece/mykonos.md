3 hours in Mykonos isn't long enough for the island, but ... it was far better than nothing. 

We did a 1-day boat ferry from Paros to Delos and Mykonos with [Polos tours](https://polostoursparos.com/daily-cruises/delos-mykonos/). It was a busy day, but it let us visit both without re-packing a suitcase.

> We didn't prioritize Mykonos because, like Santorini, it is so "overdiscovered".  But we now know why it's so well-appreciated.

Its setting by the water, coupled with white-washed buildings and narrow car-free lanes makes for a delightful spot to wander.

![caption 05](images/Mykonos-05.jpg)

The iconic 16th-century windmills attract attention. 

![Windmill close-up](images/Mykonos-06.jpg)


![The iconic windmills of Mykonos](images/Mykonos-01.jpg)

The area near the port is filled with sophisticated boutiques. Tiffany very much enjoyed the shopping opportunities. 

![The haul from Mykonos](images/Mykonos-07.jpg)

The wines at Moraitis winery were some of the better ones we had in the Cycladic islands.

![Winery near Mykonos town](images/Mykonos-02.jpg)

The ancient protective walls are filled with bars, many with small openings carved to see the harbor.

![View of the port through ancient wall](images/Mykonos-03.jpg)

Sakis Grill House was the top search result for "best gyro in Mykonos", and its gyros and souvlaki earned the crowds.

:::nutshell Mykonos
verdict: Glad We Went
duration: 3 hours
Stay Overnight: We didn't -- three hours was enough to see why people love it, not enough to really explore.
Don't Miss: The iconic windmills; the shopping (per Tiffany).
Best Time of Day: Can't say from three hours -- we had midday.
Worth the Splurge: TBD -- we didn't stay long enough to judge.
Return Visit: Maybe. It's gorgeous, but very over-discovered.
:::
